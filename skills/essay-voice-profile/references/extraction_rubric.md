# Extraction Rubric

Use this checklist while building `voice_model`.

## Coverage

- Use at least three essays when available.
- Capture patterns that recur across samples, not one-off quirks.
- Include 5-8 excerpts and diversify selection:
  - Opening or framing paragraph
  - Core argument paragraph
  - Transition-heavy paragraph
  - Synthesis or counterargument paragraph
  - Conclusion or call-to-action paragraph

## Field Guidance

- `summary`: Describe the full voice profile in one paragraph with concrete traits.
- `tone`: Label register and stance directly (for example: formal-analytic with occasional conversational pivots).
- `sentence_patterns`: Note average cadence, complexity, punctuation habits, and variation.
- `argument_style`: Explain claim progression and evidence handling.
- `vocabulary_level`: Describe lexical range and preferred diction.
- `rhetorical_devices`: Name repeated devices and tie each to observed behavior.
- `transition_patterns`: Document connector words and discourse flow.
- `paragraph_structure`: State the dominant structure template.
- `distinctive_phrases`: Keep literal phrases short and exact.
- `what_to_avoid`: Convert anti-patterns into actionable constraints.

## Quality Checks

- Avoid generic descriptors without evidence.
- Avoid invented quotations or fabricated citations.
- Keep excerpts verbatim and non-overlapping.
- Ensure each `demonstrates` note explains a distinct signal.
- If styles conflict across essays, explain the mixed profile explicitly.
